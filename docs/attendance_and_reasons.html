Key Patterns and Ministry Insights
71+ Age Group Dynamics

The senior adult group consistently cites "preaching and biblical teaching" as their primary reason for choosing West Franklin. When asked what stands out, they frequently mention "friendliness" and "worship experience." Their long-term attendance pattern (57.2% attending 4+ years) suggests they find deep value in these core aspects of church life.

56-70 Age Group Perspectives

This group shows the strongest medium-term commitment (45.2% in 4-7 year range) and often mentions "community feel" and "size of congregation" as key factors in their choice. They particularly value the balance of traditional and contemporary elements, with many noting the "family atmosphere" as a standout feature.

41-55 Age Group Priorities

Recent growth in this age group (34.8% joining in last 1-3 years) appears driven by the church's strong biblical teaching and family ministry opportunities. They frequently cite "preaching style" and "opportunities for involvement" as standout features, suggesting they value both spiritual depth and active participation.

26-40 Age Group Motivations

Young families in this group overwhelmingly mention "community" and "children's programs" as their main reasons for choosing West Franklin. The strong showing in the 4-7 year attendance range (42.1%) correlates with positive feedback about "welcoming atmosphere" and "family-focused activities" as standout features.

19-25 Age Group Experience

Though the smallest group, these young adults consistently mention "community" and "teaching" as their primary draws. Their feedback emphasizes the importance of both spiritual growth opportunities and social connections, suggesting potential growth areas for young adult ministry.

Addressing Needs of Newer Members by Age Group (Those who have been attending 3yrs or less: "Recent Joiners")
71+ Recent Joiners (15 members, 42.8% of age group)

Common concerns center around physical accessibility and connection. Many mention transportation challenges and difficulty attending evening events. Several note feeling "out of touch" with technology used for church communications.

Recommended Actions:
  • Establish a transportation ministry specifically for senior adults
  • Offer regular technology assistance sessions
  • Ensure printed materials remain available alongside digital communications
  • Create daytime fellowship opportunities
56-70 Recent Joiners (11 members, 35.4% of age group)

This group expresses desire for deeper involvement but often feels uncertain about where they fit. Several mention feeling "too young" for senior activities but "too old" for family-focused events. Some note frustration with life group options that don't match their schedule or life stage.

Recommended Actions:
  • Create specific ministry roles that leverage their experience
  • Develop mixed-age life groups focused on shared interests rather than life stage
  • Provide clear pathways for involvement in mentoring younger families
  • Offer more flexible life group scheduling options
41-55 Recent Joiners (10 members, 43.4% of age group)

Primary concerns revolve around juggling church involvement with busy family schedules. Several single members in this age group mention feeling "overlooked" in a family-focused church. Some express desire for more targeted support with parenting teenagers.

Recommended Actions:
  • Create specific ministry opportunities for singles in this age range
  • Develop support groups for parents of teenagers
  • Offer more flexible serving opportunities that accommodate varying schedules
  • Consider childcare availability for evening activities
26-40 Recent Joiners (7 members, 36.9% of age group)

Young families express challenges with childcare during church activities and desire for more consistent children's programming. Several mention feeling overwhelmed by expectations to serve while managing young families. Some note difficulty connecting beyond surface level in life groups.

Recommended Actions:
  • Expand childcare availability for church activities
  • Create more family-inclusive serving opportunities
  • Develop shorter-term commitment options for ministry involvement
  • Structure life groups to allow deeper sharing and support
19-25 Recent Joiners (2 members, 100% of age group)

Though a small group, they express feeling disconnected from their age peers and uncertain about their role in the church. They mention wanting more opportunities for meaningful involvement beyond youth ministry but feeling intimidated by adult programs.

Recommended Actions:
  • Create specific young adult fellowship opportunities
  • Develop mentoring connections with slightly older adults
  • Provide clear pathways for involvement in church leadership
  • Consider partnering with other churches for young adult activities
